Knuckle Normals

To get the most out of your PA rotations, you’ll need to understand how your normal attacks interact with your rotations, as doing so will enable you to be more PP efficient and have higher up time.
Knuckles have 3 attacks that regenerate PP:

Normal 1: 5 PP
Normal 2: 7 PP
Normal 3: 13 PP

Each normal or PA advances the combo by one action. Keeping track of these actions in a series of normal attacks is crucial to keeping damage and PP optimized during a rotation. In general, Normal 3 is the highest damage and most PP efficient of a Normal series. As such, Normal 3 can be weaved into a combo like as follows:

From Neutral: PA → PA → Normal 3 → PA → PA → Normal 3
From Neutral: Normal 1 → PA → PA → PA → PA → PA → Normal 3

Using Step or Step Attack will return the player to neutral, allowing you to reset your combo if needed. Additionally, Step or Step Attack will hold TAJA while you have Knuckle Gear



Photon Arts

Backhand Smash (40 PP)
(ONE PUNCH! But, more like 20 times.)

Knuckles' core bossing PA. A hard hitting,quick animation with extremely limited range. Useful against slow moving or knocked down enemies. Has VERY HIGH KNOCKBACK and sends small enemies FLYING.

Flash Thousand (20 PP)
(ORA ORA ORA ORA ORA ORA!)

Useful against enemies that Backhand Smash isn't cut out for, such as mobile targets or enemies with weird hitboxes.

Maintains altitude and has FRONTAL guard frames during the uppercut

Pendulumn Roll (30 PP)
(Go watch Hajime No Ippo)

Useful against enemies that Backhand Smash isn't cut out for, such as mobile targets or enemies with weird hitboxes.

Maintains altitude and has FRONTAL guard frames during the uppercut

Quake Howling (15 PP)
(Earthquake the TM)

Knuckles' core mobbing PA. A PP efficient PA that stuns mobs. Has super armor for its full duration.
Gains a larger AoE and slight tracking when used mid-air.

Slide Upper (20 PP) (Uncharged/Uncrafted)
(Just use Type-0 instead)

The uncharged variant of this PA should be used when you don't have enough time to charge it. Sometimes used against flimsy mobs.

Slide Upper Type-0 (10~20 PP)
(The bargain at the supermarket is today!)

A chargeable PA that's primarily used as a preemptive attack.

Should be charged and ready to release while waiting for an enemy to spawn or come towards you.

Maintains height if you cancel out of it towards the end of the PA. Great for retaining DPS in downtime and keeping Knuckle Gear up if you're aiming for an airborne mob.

Flicker Jab (10 PP)
(Consecutive normal punches)

Great as a setup for Backhand Smash due to its quick speed and low PP cost. Also used as fodder for Tech Arts Count Bonus.

Ducking Blow (8 PP)
(Nice try, big guy)

Extremely PP efficient PA that's mainly used as a defensive alternative to Flicker Jab. Also useful against aggressive humanoid enemies as it's basically a better sway.

Heartless Impact (15 PP)
(No, not Genshin Impact)

Great as a travel PA or gap closer. Has guard frames on startup. Dosen't lose elevation when used mid-air.

Suprise Knuckle (15PP)
(Suprise! It's not one, but 3 fists!)

A multi-purpose PA often used when Backhand Smash isn't viable. Very quick PA; while grounded, provider Super Armor and moves you towards your target.

Straight Charge (Uncrafted) (20 PP)
(Don't use this.)

Uncrafted, Straight Charge is straight garbage.

Straight Charge Type-0 (15~25 PP)
(THIS IS THE FIST THAT WILL PIERCE THE HEAVENS!)

Used to pursue moving targets. Deals continuous damage that increases to its maximum after six hits. Mainly used for keeping up with targets that are moving.

Meteor Fist (30PP)
(Learning how to meteor right)

Calls a fist down from the sky onto your target. The size of the fist is RNG based. The large fist does the most damage, has the biggest aoe, while being the rarest. Small fist does poor damage, has the smallest aoe. Medium fist rains down small fists, does alright damage, and has a medium aoe.

Fun fact: This PA doesn't gain bonuses from Fighter's TAJA skills, but does from Etoiles TACB. Good job, Sega



Knuckle PA Rotations

Knuckles have strange rotations. There are a few set combos that work really well, but at the end of the day, you'll need to vary your PA usage to fit your current situation. With that in mind, here are some basic combos to get you started.
Flicker Jab > Backhand Smash > N3

A basic knuckle rotation. Used when your target is either extremely slow moving, or completely immobile. Has higher DPS than other Knuckle combos because of either Flicker Jab's quick animation, but is often unreliable due to Backhand Smash's range. If you're using Etoile sub you should aim to use Backhand Smash three times in a row to fully utilize TACB. You can also sub out Flicker Jab for Ducking Blow if you need a PA with better guard frames.


Suprise Knuckle > Pendulum Roll

Used in situations where you need to be defensive against quick, aggressive enemies due to suprise Knuckles' super armor.


Heartless Impact > Flash Thousand

This is your gapcloser/chase combo that's better for fighting enemies that has their weak point in the air and is slow or stationary. These two PAs won't lose you any airborne time as the JA window's that they both share appear before the animation is complete.

You can also sub in Backhand Smash if you're confident that it can connect.


Quake Howling > Quake Howling

Your bread and butter mobbing combo. Useful it's low PP cost, large AOE, and ability to stun enemies. Repeated casts of Quake Howling are particularly PP efficient when using Etoile as your subclass.

Quake Howling's range can be increased by preceding it with an aerial cast of Heartless Impact.


Slide Upper 0 > Pendulum Roll > N3

Works well as a transition into other combos. Very PP efficient during Limit Break.



Sway and It's Uses

Sway is the knuckles weapon action (which you nerds should already know right?) It's main use is to quickly dodge out of the way of incoming attacks with a very quick and hard to land weave action. It can be changed to have a slower weave with a ring to make it way easier to dodge incoming attacks with a huge drawback on how you can use sway to animation cancel your PAs.

Sway Canceling

Sway canceling is rather not that complicated to pull off, all you have to do to pull off the cancel is when at the last few frames of your pa animation you hit sway to end it early. This is only possible with normal sway as slow action sway will lock you into it's slow animation until it's finished.
Also swaying in the middle of your combos can drastically decrease how quickly you lose altitude.

Complex Photon Arts

Complex PAs work differently from ordinary PAs. They’re not as complex as the name implies, but you should still know a few things about them. Firstly, Complex PAs require stocks (which you gain by dealing damage) to cast them. Secondly, you must have the correct weapon equipped in order to use its corresponding Complex PA. Finally, Complex PAs only scale with the weapon’s attack stat (both base attack and affixes count). Their damage is unaffected by the following:

  • Just Attacks

  • Percentage damage increases, such as Weapon Potentials, Ripper Affixes, Slayer affixes, Phrase affixes, and S-Class Special Abilities. S5:Anthesis Cultivation is the exception to this, and will increase the damage of Complex PAs

  • Percentage damage boosts in your skill tree, such as Limit Break

Additional Information About Complex PAs:

Complex PAs can inflict critical hits, but are not affected by critical damage boosts. (Yes, even Fighter Skills that provide crit bonuses.)

Combo-based skills such as Tech Arts JA Boost, Fury Combo, Etoile JA Bonus, JA Crit, and Tech Arts Count Bonus will not drop their bonuses when you use a Complex PA, provided you JA directly after your Complex PA cast.
Fatal Knockout (3 Stocks)

After a half second windup, unleash a devastating, multi-hit impact. If an attack is parried during the windup, the activation of this PA is faster. Extremely useful for clearing out mobs or getting extra damage in on a boss.
